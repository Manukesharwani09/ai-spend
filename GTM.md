# Go-To-Market Strategy

## The Exact Target User
target user is not a generic "startup founder." The exact persona is the **VP of Engineering or Director of Engineering at a Series A or Series B startup (50–150 employees)**. 

At this specific stage, the startup has outgrown the phase where founders know everyone's tech stack, and they have recently hired their first dedicated finance hire (or fractional CFO) who is actively auditing the corporate credit card. The VP of Eng is tasked with "cleaning up the SaaS bloat" but lacks visibility into exactly which AI tools overlap and what the benchmark spend should be.

## What They Search and Scroll
Right before they need this tool, they are Googling high-intent bottom-of-funnel queries:
- *"Cursor Business vs Enterprise pricing"*
- *"Claude Team plan vs API direct"*
- *"How to track OpenAI API spend across teams"*

They are actively scrolling LinkedIn reading hot takes about SaaS bloat, or browsing Hacker News threads titled *"Ask HN: What is your team's monthly AI spend per developer?"*

## Where They Hang Out
- **Slack Communities:** Rands Leadership Slack (specifically the `#cto-chatter` and `#engineering-management` channels), CTO Connection, and YC Founders Slack (if applicable).
- **Subreddits:** `r/ExperiencedDevs` and `r/SaaS`. 
- **Newsletters:** Pragmatic Engineer (Gergely Orosz) and TLDR Web Dev.

## Getting the First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)
Will bypass generic Twitter shouting and use highly targeted, high-effort organic channels:

1. **The Hacker News "State of AI Pricing" Drop:** Will write a high-effort engineering blog post analyzing how startups overpay for AI (e.g., exposing that a 10-person team pays $3,000/yr for ChatGPT Plus when their aggregate API usage would cost $400/yr). Will link the SignalSpend audit tool at the bottom as a free calculator to check their own stack.
2. **Cold Outbound to Fractional CFOs:** Will search LinkedIn for "Fractional CFO" catering to tech startups. Will DM 100 of them offering the tool as a free, white-labeled value-add they can send to their clients to look smart. *("Hey [Name], built a quick calculator to audit AI vendor bloat. Might be useful for your Q2 audits with clients.")*
3. **The "Roast My Stack" Reddit Strategy:** Will post anonymous, authentic threads in `r/ExperiencedDevs` asking for feedback on a $2k/mo AI bill, posting a screenshot of the SignalSpend results page, inherently driving curious engineering managers to the tool to test their own numbers.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
Credex already possesses a massive, unfair advantage: a CRM full of "closed-lost" leads or cold prospects who previously bounced because they didn't think they were spending enough to warrant buying discounted AI credits. Credex can deploy a single email blast to these 5,000+ dormant leads: *"We built a free calculator to see if you even need our credits yet. Find out in 60 seconds."* This weaponizes dead leads into active top-of-funnel traffic.

## Week-1 Traction Goals
If this GTM playbook executes correctly, Week 1 traction looks like:
- **Unique Visitors:** 450
- **Audits Completed:** 85 (18% conversion rate from landing to result)
- **Consultations Booked:** 6 high-intent leads captured for Credex.
